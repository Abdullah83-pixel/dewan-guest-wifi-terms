4.0DAE Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy

4.1User Acceptance & Agreement

4.1.1Context

DAE’s voluntary BYOD program allows staff to use their Personal Mobile Devices (PMD’s) to access company systems and email. The program will not affect DAE’s allocation of equipment in PC’s, laptops and company provided mobile devices.

DAE will manage this program through a Mobile Device Management Platform (MDM) Staff, Individuals will be defined as full time, and part time employees, DAE appointed contractors, DAE consultants, sub consultants, and temporary staff.

4.1.2Acceptance of policy

Staff who wish to register their PMD under the BYOD framework will accept all terms and conditions in this policy. Staff who connect any PMD device up to the DAEBYOD or Guest wireless networks also accept the terms of this policy upon login.

Acceptance of this policy is also acceptance of the wider framework of DAE Guest/BYOD Wi-Fi Terms of Service and IT Acceptable Use Policy. Any breaches of this policy will result in the individual’s termination from the BYOD program and may result in disciplinary action under the staff code of conduct policies.

4.1.3Data Responsibility / Backups

DAE IT is not responsible for the backup or recovery of data on any PMD used within the BYOD framework. The responsibility for any personal data remains with the employee. If it is required to delete data from a PMD, DAE will not compensate for that loss of data. DAE will not install any application software (e.g. iTunes) on DAE assets for the purpose of backing up PMD data.

4.2DAE / Employer Device Control Mobile Device Management Platform (MDM)

Individual that wish to utilize BYOD services will need to register with DAE’s *MDM solution. DAE will push out security policies, service configurations, and recommended applications to the device.

By default, DAE will also have access to other information on the PMD including device hardware details, GPS data, geotracking information, operating system, other installed applications, mobile service numbers, and location services on that device.

DAE will have the ability to remove the corporate data pushed to that device or completely wipe the PMD if required. DAE also reserves the right to remove any individual from the BYOD program or remove applications from a PMD determined to be a security risk to the organization.

4.3Data Privacy

DAE’s *MDM solution and data is restricted to designated IT personnel for audit, support and compliance. Data from this system can only be utilized by approval from the DAE Senior Management.

4.4Legal

Individuals who utilize the BYOD network must be aware that in the case of legal action or police investigations PMD’s may be confiscated as a result of an inquiry or criminal investigation as per the external authorities’ processes.

DAE reserves the right to request the termination of an individual’s access to the DAE BYOD and Guest networks.

4.5PMD Lost, broken or stolen devices

DAE is not responsible for any damages, insurance, theft or loss of PMD’s in its offices. Any loss or damage to personal devices is the individual’s responsibility, and suitable insurance or protection is the responsibility of the individual. Staff members can request, via the service desk, location or device wiping services. If a device is stolen or lost, the *MDM can be utilized to determine the approximate location of the device, and/or remote wipe the device.

4.6PMD device costs

PMD usage, and all associated costs and charges are the responsibility of the individual unless otherwise agreed with the Company. Any staff member that is using a PMD does so at his or her own choice. DAE is not responsible for any associated costs of a staff PMD. This includes but is not limited to any insurance, damages, warranty, and legal, data plans, exceeding data caps, PSD memory, accessories & peripherals.

4.7Support

4.7.1Best Effort Support Model

DAE IT will provide a "Best Effort Support Model" in regards to individuals PMD’s. Best effort is defined as DAE IT providing reasonable local and remote assistance for staff. Under the support model, the following services are supported:

  • Troubleshooting for wireless connectivity to the guest and BYOD networks.
  • Configuration and login to the DAE email systems.
  • Configuration and login to the DAE VPN Solution.
  • All other support and application issues with a PMD remain the responsibility of the employee using a PMD.

4.7.2Application Support

DAE’s standard operating environment and applications suite will only be supported via DAE’s VPN remote desktop and application solution. Access to all applications, e.g., Revit, AutoCAD etc. will only be supported under this platform. Please note that at this stage most of these applications are not suited for the small form factor of smartphones.

4.7.3Application Costs

DAE will not pay for or reimburse any application costs for a PMD device. DAE is under no obligation to cover the application costs as the program is voluntary. This includes email clients for the PMD.

4.7.4Preferred Models

DAE Preferred Models of PMD for ease of support include:

4.7.4.1Apple IPhone & IPad utilizing IOS 12.1 or above
4.7.4.2Windows 10 (Laptop)
4.7.4.3Mac OSX Lion (MacBook)
4.7.4.4Android 9.1 tablet & smartphone

4.7.5Community Support & Workshops

DAE has limited support capabilities for BYOD. All documentation will be provided on the Intranet and on the wireless networks login page. (Still to be completed!) DAE Technology will also periodically host workshops where staff can enquire about services and setup under the BYOD program. Community support will also be available in the future on the Intranet, where BYOD users can post information on self-service solutions.

4.7.6Loan Pool Devices

DAE will not have a loan pool of devices in the case of loss or damage to a PMD.

4.8User Etiquette

Staff utilizing a PMD should observe use restrictions and appropriate etiquette to ensure safety, courtesy, respect and privacy for themselves and others. As PMD’s can create a disturbance, individuals should ensure appropriate and discrete use of their PMD to minimize the inconvenience to others and ensure their conversations remain private.

4.9Security (Physical, Data & Network)

4.9.1 Staff using a PMD and accessing DAE data or email shall ensure the physical security of the device and immediately report any loss, theft or damage to Regional IT Manager, and where appropriate to the police and report if the device contains any corporate data or email. If the PMD and/or related equipment are subsequently found, the user must immediately notify those parties originally advised of the loss or theft.

4.9.2 PMD owners shall ensure the physical security of the device if it contains any DAE data or emails or data acquired through 3rd parties. Owners must ensure that all DAE data is permanently removed from devices prior to leaving the organization. DAE also reserves the right to data wipe the PMD if deemed appropriate.
4.9.3 Staff using a PMD and accessing DAE data or email will have device password/PIN and timeout setting established on the device pushed out via the *MDM policy manager. The time out setting the device to go into lock mode must not exceed 10 minutes. It is a requirement that owners of private mobile devices that contain DAE data do not alter or disable this policy setting.

4.9.4 DAE reserves the right to interrogate PMD’s that attempt to connect to the DAE Network or Email system. Devices deemed a security risk would be prevented from accessing DAE’s Network.

4.9.5 Some mobile devices contain portable storage devices (PSD) such as flash memory cards that can be used to store data. Individuals are to ensure the physical security of privately owned PSD’s if they contain DAE data. Staff should consider implementing data encryption on PSD’s dependent on the nature of the data stored. DAE Technology can provide advice in terms of encryption options.

4.9.6 Sensitive DAE data is not to be stored on PMD’s. This includes data relating to, but not limited to, sensitive staff matters, personal details, client inquiries, 3rd party information, financial information, organizational planning, budget papers etc. Staff must consult with their Line Manager if they are not sure what constitutes sensitive data.

4.9.7 Network security credentials (e.g. username and password) must not be stored locally on the PMD unless authorized by the Regional IT Manager. This includes both DAE issued and privately owned mobile devices.

4.9.8 PMD’s are only permitted to access the wireless networks designated for use by DAE Technology. PMD’s are not permitted to be connected up to or plugged into any physical network point in the organisation.

4.9.9 PMD’s owners are to ensure that devices are updated regularly and should update applications and operating systems to the latest approved updates and security patches. Jail broken IOS or root broken Android Devices will not be permitted to connect to the *MDM and associated DAE services.
